Presidential Approval Ratings and the Foreign Exchange Market: The Korean Won under the Park Guen Hye Government

Abstract

Do presidential approval ratings affect exchange rates? The empirical purview of the vast literature on this topic has been confined to the run-up to elections. The importance of approval ratings in non-election periods has therefore been under-studied. Examining daily data on the exchange rate of the Korean won during the presidency of Park Geun Hye, we find that the won weakened (1) when Parkâ€™s ratings were low and (2) when they bounced back unexpectedly from a low level.
